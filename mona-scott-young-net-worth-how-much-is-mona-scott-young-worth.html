Early Life and Music Manager

Mona Scott-Young, born on February 15, 1967, in New York City, spent her formative years immersed in the vibrant music scene of the city. Raised by her Haitian parents, she developed a passion for music and honed her business acumen from an early age.

After attending Park West High School, Scott-Young faced the need to work and cover her rent, and as a result, she decided not to pursue a college education. This led her to dive headfirst into the music industry, where she quickly made a name for herself as a talented music manager.

Scott-Young’s rise to prominence in the industry began when she took on the role of manager for the Brooklyn-based group TrackMasters. Her expertise and keen eye for talent helped establish the careers of numerous artists, including Busta Rhymes, LL Cool J, and Missy Elliott.

“Mona Scott-Young played a pivotal role in the success of many artists, reshaping the landscape of the music industry.”

Driven to create a lasting impact in the music world, Scott-Young co-founded Violator Management with Chris Lighty. The company became instrumental in launching and revitalizing the careers of artists across various genres. From managing reputations to securing record deals, Scott-Young brought her unrivaled expertise to the table, solidifying her reputation as an indispensable force in the industry.

Reality TV Empire

After achieving success as a talent manager with Violator, Mona Scott-Young took the leap into television production. She began her television career as a producer on “The Road to Stardom with Missy Elliott.” The experience further fueled her passion for the industry, inspiring her to establish her own television production company, Monami Productions.

In 2006, Mona Scott-Young created the groundbreaking reality TV show “Love & Hip Hop.” This trailblazing series delves into the lives of hip-hop artists, showcasing their music, struggles, relationships, and aspirations.
