Andrew McCutchen is an American professional baseball player with an estimated net worth of $19 million.

Andrew McCutchen started to play baseball when he was an eight grader for Fort Meade High School. During his senior season, he batted .474, .709 with 16 home runs, and 42 RBIs. Aside from baseball, he also played football and ran track.

He signed a letter of intent to play football for the University of Florida, and as of the same time he was selected as the 11th overall pick in the 2005 MLB Draft by the Pittsburgh Pirates. McCutchen took the starting position during the 2006 South Atlantic League's All-Star team.  That same year, he was named as the Minor League Player of the Year.

McCutchen called up to the major league in 2009. He was a three-time Silver Slugger awardee, and received the Gold Glove award in 2012. During the 2013 season, he won the National League MVP.

Andrew Stefan McCutchen was born on October 10, 1986 in Fort Meade, Florida.
