Hans Zimmer

Birthname Hans Florian Zimmer Birthdate September 12, 1957 Birthplace Frankfurt am Main, West Germany Education Hurtwood House school Profession Musician Net worth $90,000,000 Source of Wealth Music Nationality German, American Country Germany Marital Status Married (Suzanne) Children 4

Hans Zimmer is a German film composer and music producer who has an estimated net worth value of $90 million. Zimmer started pursuing his career when he moved to London. He began by composing music for advertisements. Then, he went on to collaborate with "The Buggles" in 1979 for their "Age of Plastic" album.

After 1980, he met Stanley Myers, and started working with him created the Lillie Yard Studio in London. Zimmer composed his first movie scores in the year of 1988, for British movies such as "A World Apart" and "Burning Secret". He gained attention from the famous film producer Barry Levinson for his role in the "Rain Man" movie. At the beginning of the 1990's, he composed music for famous Hollywood movies such as "Black Rain" and "Days of Thunder". Zimmer then collaborated with Elton John for the soundtrack of the "Lion King" movie and won his first Oscar. He also got a place in the "Top 100 Living Geniuses" of the Daily Telegraph. Zimmer earned his fame for composing the scores of numerous movies, out of which many really famous such as "Gladiator", "The Dark Night", "Hannibal" and others.
