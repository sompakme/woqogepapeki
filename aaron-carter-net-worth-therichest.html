Aaron Charles Carter is an American singer that has an estimated of around -$2 million. Aaron Carter started out as a pop and R&B singer in the early 90's, among the most popular stars at the time.

Aaron's singing career started when he became the lead singer of the Dead End Band at the age of seven. Later, following his older into to the spotlight, he entered into his solo singing career. His first solo appearance was when he was 9, when he opened for his brother Nick Carter's band: Backstreet Boys in Berlin in March 1997.

Carter has also dabbled into the realm of acting. His TV appearances include "Sabrina, the Teenage Witch" (1996), "7th Heaven" (1996) and "Lizzie McGuire" (2001).

Carter guest starred in season 9 of Dancing with the Stars back in  2009, he was paired up with Karina Smirnoff. The pair were eliminated, finishing in fifth place.
