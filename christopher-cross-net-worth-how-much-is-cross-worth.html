“Arthur’s Theme” – Christopher Cross’s Academy Award-Winning Hit

One of Christopher Cross’s most iconic and successful songs is “Arthur’s Theme (Best That You Can Do).” Co-written with music legends Burt Bacharach, Carole Bayer Sager, and Peter Allen, this song was featured in the 1981 film “Arthur,” starring Dudley Moore and Liza Minnelli. The catchy and heartfelt tune resonated with audiences, and it quickly climbed the charts, reaching the number one spot on the Billboard Hot 100.

“Arthur’s Theme” captured the essence of the film and became an instant classic. Its memorable lyrics and Cross’s smooth vocals struck a chord with listeners worldwide. The song’s message of finding hope and love in difficult times resonated with audiences, making it a timeless anthem.”

In addition to its chart success, “Arthur’s Theme (Best That You Can Do)” garnered critical acclaim and went on to win Cross an Academy Award for Best Original Song. This prestigious recognition solidified Cross’s place in music history and highlighted his exceptional songwriting abilities.
